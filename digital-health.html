Special Focus

Digital
Health

In inter- and transdisciplinary projects, meaningful results depend on whether disciplines can actually work together — across different languages, assumptions, and standards of evidence.

The challenge

Interdisciplinary by nature

Digital health draws together clinicians, data scientists, engineers, mathematicians, and domain specialists — often working across deeply different languages, assumptions, and professional cultures. In projects that span these boundaries, deliberate integration work is what allows the different contributions to cohere into a shared scientific whole.

"In inter- and transdisciplinary projects, integration is not a coordination task — it is a scientific one."
Why it matters

Where connections break down

In inter- and transdisciplinary digital health projects, data must be correctly interpreted across fields, models must reflect clinical reality, and results must be meaningful to both technical and medical communities. Without deliberate integration, these connections break down — not because the science is weak, but because the bridging work was never made explicit.

Making integration a defined function from the outset strengthens scientific coherence and prevents the misalignments that become delays, duplication, or incompatible outputs.

Integration domains

Where integration expertise matters

Digital health projects span a wide range of integration challenges — from aligning technical and clinical perspectives to navigating the regulatory, ethical, and stakeholder dimensions that shape what research can actually deliver.

Clinical & technical alignment
Bridging medical knowledge and computational methods
Data infrastructure
Collection, curation, and interoperability
Patient engagement
Integrating patient perspectives and lived experience
Regulatory aspects
Navigating approval, compliance, and governance frameworks
Ethics & data protection
Privacy, consent, and responsible use of health data
