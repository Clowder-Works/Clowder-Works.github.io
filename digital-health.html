The challenge

Interdisciplinary by nature

Digital health is an inherently inter- and transdisciplinary domain. Clinicians, data scientists, engineers, mathematicians, and domain specialists must work together — often across deeply different languages, assumptions, and working cultures. No single discipline can deliver meaningful results alone.

"Integration is not a nice-to-have. It is a core scientific requirement."
Why it matters

Integration as scientific necessity

Data must be correctly interpreted across fields. Models must reflect clinical reality. Results must be meaningful to both technical and medical communities. Without deliberate integration, these connections break down.

This makes integration not an administrative or coordination task — it is an intellectual one. Making it explicit from the outset strengthens scientific coherence and prevents the misalignments that become delays or incompatible outputs.

Illustrative example

Digital twins in medicine

Building a virtual human twin requires computational modelling, clinical knowledge, data infrastructure, and validation across disciplines — all of which must be tightly integrated to produce results that are scientifically sound and practically useful.

Mathematical and simulation foundations
