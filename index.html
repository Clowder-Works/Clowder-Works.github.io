Why integration matters

The gap that project management doesn't fill

In large collaborative projects, success depends not only on strong disciplines and sound administration, but on whether the different work packages are designed to produce a coherent scientific whole.

Project management is essential for timelines, coordination, and reporting. Yet integration is a separate intellectual task: aligning concepts, translating across fields, and connecting methods, data, and stakeholder perspectives into a shared research architecture.

Without a dedicated integration function, cross-disciplinary alignment often becomes hidden labour — absorbed at leadership level, invisible in the proposal, and costly to recover mid-project. Making integration explicit from the outset strengthens proposal credibility and frees Principal Investigators to focus on scientific leadership.

What we do

Three ways Clowder Works supports your project

01 — Proposal development

Design the integration logic before reviewers ask for it

For Horizon Europe and national collaborative calls, Clowder Works helps design how disciplines connect, where stakeholder input enters, how knowledge will be translated across domains, and how integration is built into work packages rather than left implicit. Complex consortia become easier for reviewers to assess — and more credible.

02 — Implementation

Structured alignment through the life of the project

Facilitated workshops, communication structures, and reflection formats that surface misalignments early — before they become delays, duplication, or scientifically incompatible outputs.

03 — Training & capacity

Institutional capacity for interdisciplinary research

Training for universities, doctoral schools, and project teams in interdisciplinary and transdisciplinary collaboration, facilitation, and stakeholder engagement — building lasting capacity, not generic team development.
