Research integration

What is research integration

Integration turns separate expertise into something new.

It brings concepts, methods, data, and stakeholder knowledge into one coherent research design.

It helps people across fields understand each other, work through mismatches, and build a shared way of thinking.

The result is research that no single discipline could produce alone.

A project manager keeps the project moving. An integrator makes the science work together.

Why it matters

Why research integration matters

Integration matters because it helps a collaboration produce more than a collection of separate contributions.

It creates shared understanding across disciplines and connects different kinds of expertise.

Even more, it turns diversity in methods, data, and perspectives into new scientific value.

That makes projects more coherent, proposals more credible, and outcomes more relevant to research and practice.

It also gives Principal Investigators the space to focus on scientific leadership, while integration is handled as a recognised part of the work.

What we do

What Clowder Works does

01

Proposal development

Clowder Works designs the integration logic of your proposal.

That means showing reviewers how disciplines connect, where stakeholder knowledge enters, and how integration is built into work packages — not left implicit.

For Horizon Europe and national calls, this increasingly determines whether an ambitious proposal is seen as credible and deliverable.

02

Support during implementation

Clowder Works provides structured support for alignment across work packages, disciplines, and stakeholder groups.

This includes facilitated workshops, communication structures, and reflection formats that surface misalignments early, before they become delays, duplication, or scientifically incompatible outputs.

03

Training and capacity building

Clowder Works offers training for universities, doctoral schools, and project teams in interdisciplinary and transdisciplinary collaboration, facilitation, integration frameworks, and stakeholder engagement.

The purpose is not generic team development, but stronger institutional capacity to design and run scientifically coherent collaborative research.

How to engage

Collaboration pathways

01

Institutional programmes

Workshops and structured training for university faculties, medical schools, doctoral schools, and PhD networks on interdisciplinary collaboration, integration methods, and cross-disciplinary communication.

Designed for research leadership seeking to build this capacity across their institution rather than project by project.

02

Team-level collaboration

Tailored, in-depth work with individual research teams: integration design, facilitated alignment, and structured support through proposal development or project implementation.

03

Proposal partnership

Embedded collaboration on a specific funding application, co-developing the integration logic, work package structure, and stakeholder mapping for Horizon Europe or national calls.
