Definition

What is research integration

Research integration is the work of making disciplines, methods, data, and stakeholders genuinely work together in a collaborative research project. It is the core practice of inter- and transdisciplinary research.

It connects concepts, methods, and data across disciplinary boundaries into one coherent research design. It helps people across fields understand each other, resolve mismatches, and build shared ways of thinking.

The result is knowledge that no single discipline could produce alone — and that only emerges when integration is treated as a deliberate, methodological practice.

A project manager keeps the project on schedule. An integrator keeps the science coherent — ensuring that the work of different disciplines adds up to more than the sum of its parts.

"Integration is an often hidden but necessary work for complex research projects. "

The missing link

Why integration matters

Integration matters because it helps a collaboration produce more than a collection of separate contributions. It creates shared understanding across disciplines and connects different kinds of expertise.

Even more, it turns diversity in methods, data, and perspectives into new scientific value. That makes projects more coherent, proposals more credible, and outcomes more relevant to research and practice.

It also gives Principal Investigators the space to focus on scientific leadership, while integration is handled as a recognised part of the work.

"Integration ensures coherence and creation of new value."

The name

Why Clowder?

A clowder is the collective noun for a group of cats — an old English word for things gathered in a loose, independent bunch. It is a precise metaphor for interdisciplinary research: independent experts with different goals and strong opinions, who do not naturally move as one.

The name is inspired by Herding Cats: Integrative Leadership Strategies in Inter- and Transdisciplinary Research Programs — a paper by L. Deutsch and colleagues (Sustainability Science, 2025) — which uses the cat-herding metaphor to name what is genuinely hard about leading complex collaborative research: ensuring that the perspectives of independent specialists become truly integrated, not merely parallel.

Clowder Works takes that challenge as its core purpose.

"A clowder only moves together when someone does the integration work."
