Integration leadership & expertise

The case for integration as a discipline

Paper · Sustainability Science 20, 95–115 · 2025 · Open access

"Herding Cats": Integrative Leadership Strategies in ITD Research Programs

Deutsch, Björnsen Gurung, Defila, Di Giulio, Hoffmann & Scholz. Drawn from in-depth study of three Swiss ITD research programs, this paper identifies six core leadership challenges and makes a clear empirical case that leading an ITD programme is a specialist skill requiring distinct competencies — not generic research management.

DOI: 10.1007/s11625-024-01585-4

Paper · Humanities and Social Sciences Communications 9, 1 · 2022 · Open access

Integrate the Integrators! A Call for Establishing Academic Careers for Integration Experts

Hoffmann, Deutsch, Thompson Klein & O'Rourke. Argues that despite integration being the central challenge of ITD research, academia offers no recognised career path for people who do it. Proposes four concrete remedies and forms the intellectual foundation of the ITD Alliance's working group on integration experts.

DOI: 10.1057/s41599-022-01138-z

Blog post · i2Insights · June 2023 · Free

Towards a Theory of Change to Institutionalise Integration Experts and Expertise

The Aeschiried Integrators. Maps a concrete pathway from the current situation — where integration expertise is largely invisible — to a future in which universities establish permanent positions for integration experts, with specific milestones and typologies of cousin roles.

Read on i2Insights

Paper · PNAS 121(32) · 2024 · Open access

Slow Convergence: Career Impediments to Interdisciplinary Biomedical Research

Berkes, Marion, Milojević & Weinberg. Analyses 154,021 biomedical PhD holders over four decades to show that the most interdisciplinary researchers stop publishing within eight years — providing large-scale quantitative evidence that current career structures actively select against those who cross disciplinary boundaries.

DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2402646121

Paper · Higher Education Research & Development · 2024

Why and How Academics Become Interdisciplinary Researchers Early in Their Careers

Taylor et al. Qualitative study identifying six distinct pathways through which early- and mid-career researchers enter interdisciplinary work — all characterised by ad hoc navigation rather than deliberate institutional support.

DOI: 10.1080/07294360.2024.2332255

Survey report · Leiden Madtrics / LERU · 2021 · Free

Skill Gaps of PhD Graduates: The Largest Gaps Are in Teamwork and Social Skills

Survey of 2,193 recently graduated PhDs finding that the largest reported skills gaps are in teamwork, social skills, and project management — precisely the competencies required for collaborative, cross-disciplinary research.

Frameworks & methods

How integration actually works

Book · ANU Press · 2013 · Free open-access e-book

Disciplining Interdisciplinarity

Gabriele Bammer. Foundational text for Integration and Implementation Sciences (i2S). Builds the case for a new research discipline dedicated to synthesising knowledge across disciplines, managing unknowns, and translating research into policy and practice change.

DOI: 10.22459/DI.01.2013 · Free e-book

Blog post · i2Insights · April 2024 · Free

Integration and Implementation Sciences (i2S) 3.0: An Updated Framework

Gabriele Bammer. The latest iteration of i2S — shorter and more immediately usable than the 2013 book. Describes three domains of expertise and positions i2S specialists as the "statisticians of complexity". The best current entry point into the framework.

Read on i2Insights

Blog post · i2Insights · September 2021 · Free

Transdisciplinary Integration: A Multidimensional Interactive Process

Fam, Thompson Klein, Hoffmann, Mitchell & Pohl. An authoritative synthesis of what integration actually means in transdisciplinary research — providing a shared vocabulary for designing, describing, and evaluating integration work in research projects.

Read on i2Insights

Blog post · i2Insights · May 2017 · Free

Methods for Integration in Transdisciplinary Research

Bergmann et al. A structured catalogue of 43 integration methods grouped into seven epistemological classes. Establishes that integration is not improvised facilitation but a methodologically grounded practice with an identifiable repertoire of techniques that can be learned, adapted, and evaluated.

Read on i2Insights

Online toolbox · td-net / Swiss Academies of Arts & Sciences · Free

The td-net Toolbox for Co-Producing Knowledge

Twenty tested tools for co-producing knowledge in transdisciplinary research, covering the full project lifecycle. Bridges the gap between abstract integration frameworks and day-to-day facilitation practice.

Networks, initiatives & practice

Community and institutional resources

01

ITD Alliance — Global Alliance for Inter- and Transdisciplinarity

The global network connecting institutions and individuals working on ITD theory, method, and practice. Organises working groups and maintains curated resources covering journals, conferences, handbooks, and member publications.

Visit ITD Alliance
02

ITD Alliance Working Group: Integration Experts and Expertise

The active community developing recognition, training, and institutional frameworks for integration expertise — organised around three output-oriented projects: Value, LTTI, and Integration Tools.

Visit working group
03

i2Insights — Integration and Implementation Insights

A global, open, peer-curated blog publishing weekly contributions on concepts, methods, and frameworks for tackling complex real-world problems. The most actively maintained practical resource in the field.

Visit i2Insights
04

SHAPE-ID: Shaping Interdisciplinary Practices in Europe

EU Horizon 2020 project producing toolkits, policy briefs, and recommendations for integrating Arts, Humanities, and Social Sciences into interdisciplinary research in European settings.

Visit SHAPE-ID
05

Collaboration and Team Science: A Field Guide

National Cancer Institute, 2nd edition. Practical guide to building and managing collaborative science teams. Widely used in health and biomedical research as onboarding material for new consortium members.
