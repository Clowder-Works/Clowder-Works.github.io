Resources

Selected publications, books, and networks on inter- and transdisciplinary research, integration methods, and collaborative science. Compiled May 2026.

Integration expertise

What integration expertise is and why it matters

Foundational texts and research on what ITD integration is, who does it, and what makes it a distinct form of expertise — not generic coordination or facilitation.

Book · ANU Press · Free open-access

Disciplining Interdisciplinarity

Gabriele Bammer. The foundational text for Integration and Implementation Sciences. Builds the case for a new discipline dedicated to synthesising knowledge across fields, managing unknowns, and connecting research to policy and practice.

DOI: 10.22459/DI.01.2013

Blog post · i2Insights · Free

i2S 3.0: An Updated Framework

Gabriele Bammer. The latest iteration of Integration and Implementation Sciences — shorter and more immediately usable than the 2013 book. The best current entry point into the i2S framework and its three domains of expertise.

Read on i2Insights

Paper · Humanities and Social Sciences Communications · Open access

Integrate the Integrators!

Hoffmann, Deutsch, Thompson Klein & O'Rourke. Argues that despite integration being the central challenge of ITD research, academia offers no recognised career path for the people who do it. Proposes four concrete remedies.

DOI: 10.1057/s41599-022-01138-z

Paper · Sustainability Science 20, 95–115 · Open access

"Herding Cats": Integrative Leadership in ITD Programs

Deutsch et al. Identifies six core leadership challenges in ITD programmes and makes an empirical case that leading this work is a specialist skill — not generic research management.

DOI: 10.1007/s11625-024-01585-4

Blog post · i2Insights · Free

Towards a Theory of Change for Integration Expertise

The Aeschiried Integrators. Maps a concrete pathway toward institutionalising integration expert roles in universities, with milestones, cousin role typologies, and a framing of integration as a rigorous and distinct methodology.

Read on i2Insights

Paper · PNAS 121(32) · Open access

Slow Convergence: Career Impediments to Interdisciplinary Research

Berkes, Marion, Milojević & Weinberg. Analyses 154,021 biomedical PhD holders over four decades showing that the most interdisciplinary researchers leave publishing earliest — quantitative evidence that careers actively select against boundary-crossers.

DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2402646121

General resources & toolboxes

Communities, networks, and practical tools

The main hubs for ongoing research, methods, and community around ITD and integration — including toolkits that bridge theory and day-to-day practice.

Network · Global

ITD Alliance

The global network for inter- and transdisciplinary research. Organises working groups, curates journals, conferences, and handbooks, and connects institutions and individuals working on ITD theory and practice.

itd-alliance.org

Blog · Weekly · Free

i2Insights

A global, open, peer-curated blog publishing weekly contributions on concepts, methods, and frameworks for tackling complex problems through research. The most actively maintained practical resource in the field.

i2insights.org

Toolkit · Free

td-net Toolbox

Twenty tested tools for co-producing knowledge in transdisciplinary research, covering the full project lifecycle. Bridges abstract integration frameworks and day-to-day facilitation practice.

Open toolbox

Project · EU Horizon 2020

SHAPE-ID

EU Horizon 2020 project producing toolkits and policy briefs for integrating Arts, Humanities, and Social Sciences into interdisciplinary research in European settings.

shapeidproject.eu

Guide · National Cancer Institute · 2nd edition

Collaboration and Team Science: A Field Guide

Practical guide to building collaborative science teams. Widely used in health and biomedical research as onboarding material for new consortium members.

Blog post · i2Insights · Free

Methods for Integration in Transdisciplinary Research

Bergmann et al. A structured catalogue of 43 integration methods in seven epistemological classes. Establishes that integration is a methodologically grounded practice with an identifiable repertoire of techniques.
